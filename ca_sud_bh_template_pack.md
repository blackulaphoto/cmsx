# California SUD and Behavioral Health Documentation Template Pack

This template pack is designed for California case managers, counselors, and behavioral health staff documenting Specialty Mental Health Services (SMHS), Drug Medi-Cal (DMC), and Drug Medi-Cal Organized Delivery System (DMC-ODS) services. California DHCS does not require standardized forms for assessments, problem lists, or progress notes, but it does require specific content elements and timelines, so these templates are structured around those required elements.[cite:10]

DHCS requires progress notes for all SMHS, DMC, and DMC-ODS services, and each note must include the service type, narrative, date, duration, location, provider signature/date, ICD-10 code, CPT/HCPCS code, and next steps.[cite:10] Progress notes must generally be completed within 3 business days, crisis notes within 24 hours, and daily progress notes are required for services billed on a daily basis such as residential and day treatment services.[cite:10]

## How to use this pack

- Replace all bracketed text with program-specific or client-specific information.
- Keep wording objective, behaviorally specific, and tied to the person’s behavioral health needs.
- When relevant, connect the service to the problem list, ASAM assessment, or SMHS assessment domains.
- For telehealth services, document consent according to program policy and DHCS requirements.[cite:10]
- For group services, maintain a participant list and document each provider’s involvement if more than one provider participated.[cite:10]

## Required documentation elements

| Document type | California baseline requirements |
|---|---|
| Progress note | Type of service, narrative describing how the service addressed behavioral health need, date of service, duration, location, provider name/signature/date, ICD-10, CPT/HCPCS, next steps.[cite:10] |
| Problem list | Current symptoms, conditions, diagnoses, and/or risk factors; ongoing updates; date and provider who added/removed each problem.[cite:10] |
| SMHS assessment | Seven required domains, provider signature/date, medical necessity determination, recommendation for services.[cite:10][cite:9] |
| DMC/DMC-ODS assessment | ASAM Criteria assessment, provider signature/date, medical necessity determination, recommendation for services.[cite:10] |
| TCM care planning content | Goals, service activities, assistance needed, course of action, and transition planning documented narratively in progress notes when applicable.[cite:10] |

## Notes for customization

Programs may still have additional county, payer, EHR, or contract-specific documentation fields beyond the California minimum standards in BHIN 22-019.[cite:10] For SUD programs, ASAM remains the required multidimensional assessment framework for DMC and DMC-ODS beneficiaries, and for SMHS programs, the seven standardized assessment domains remain required.[cite:10][cite:9]

For targeted case management, required care plan content may be documented narratively inside progress notes rather than on a separate legacy treatment-plan form, but certain service types and settings still retain separate care plan or treatment plan requirements under federal or state authority.[cite:10]
