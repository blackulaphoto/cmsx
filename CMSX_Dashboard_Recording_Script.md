**For Loom / OBS / Screen Studio**

## Video Metadata

**Title:** CMSX Dashboard Walkthrough — Ember Case Management Suite

**Short Description:**
A top-to-bottom walkthrough of the CMSX Dashboard for case managers and supervisors. Learn how to read your caseload stats, track FMLA deadlines, use the module grid, and start your day the right way.

**Recommended Length:** 8–12 minutes

**Key Teaching Points:**
- The Dashboard is your daily starting point, not a data entry screen
- Four stats cards give instant caseload visibility
- The FMLA panel has clickable status links that pre-filter the FMLA Tracker
- Notes, Docs, Bookmarks, and Resources are your personal pinboard
- The Supervisor Mode button sends supervisors to a separate workspace
- Available Services is your navigation hub to all modules
- The AI Assistant button is always accessible from any screen

## Recording Script

---

### INTRO

**Narration:**
"Welcome to the CMSX Dashboard — the home screen of the Ember Case Management Suite. This is the first screen you see every time you log in, and it's designed to answer one question: what needs your attention today? In this walkthrough, I'm going to take you through every section of the Dashboard from top to bottom so you know exactly what you're looking at and how to use it."

**Action:**
Show the full Dashboard on screen. Do not scroll yet. Let the viewer take in the full layout for 3 to 4 seconds.

**Pause:** 3 seconds of still screen before moving on.

---

### Step 1: The Header — Logo, Navigation, and User Role

**Narration:**
"At the very top of the screen you'll see the Ember logo on the left — that's your home button. Clicking it from anywhere in the app brings you back to this Dashboard. Next to it is the main navigation bar. You'll see the most commonly used modules listed here: Dashboard, Case Management, Documentation, Housing, Sober Living, and Groups. There's also a 'More' button that expands the full list of all available modules. We'll see those in detail shortly."

**Action:**
Move the cursor slowly from the Ember logo across the navigation links: Dashboard → Case Management → Documentation → Housing → Sober Living → Groups → More. Do not click any of them. Hover briefly on each to show they are clickable links.

**Narration (continuing):**
"In the top right corner, you can see the name and role of whoever is currently logged in. Right now you're looking at Brandon Vasquez, logged in as Admin / Supervisor. If you were a standard case manager, you'd see your name and the role 'Case Manager' here instead. The Logout button is right next to it."

**Action:**
Move cursor to the top-right area, pointing at "Admin / Supervisor" and "Brandon Vasquez." Then point at the Logout button. Do not click it.

**Pause:** 2 seconds.

---

### Step 2: The Dashboard Header and Subtitle

**Narration:**
"Below the top nav, the page title reads 'Dashboard.' The subtitle underneath explains the purpose of this screen: it's for reviewing caseload activity, linked workspace notes, and service modules — all without cluttering the screen with primary action controls. In other words, the Dashboard is where you see what's happening. You go into the individual modules to actually do the work."

**Action:**
Move cursor to the "Dashboard" heading, then down to the subtitle text. Slow, deliberate movement — don't rush.

**Pause:** 2 seconds.

---

### Step 3: Caseload Stats Cards

**Narration:**
"The four cards at the top of the main content area are your caseload summary. Let me walk through each one. 'Total Clients' shows the total number of clients in your system. The label underneath — '+12% this month' — gives you a trend indicator so you know if your caseload is growing."

**Action:**
Point cursor to the "Total Clients" card.

**Narration (continuing):**
"'Active Cases' shows how many cases are currently open. 'High activity' is the descriptor here. If you ever see this number elevated and the descriptor says 'Needs attention,' that's a signal to prioritize."

**Action:**
Move cursor to "Active Cases" card.

**Narration (continuing):**
"'High Risk' is the card that demands the most attention when it's nonzero. Any client flagged as high risk shows up in this count. 'Needs attention' tells you there's something urgent here."

**Action:**
Move cursor to "High Risk" card. Pause here a beat longer than the other cards.

**Narration (continuing):**
"And 'Recent Intakes' shows new clients who came into the system this week. This helps you know whether you have new clients waiting on an initial outreach."

**Action:**
Move cursor to "Recent Intakes" card.

**Narration (continuing):**
"Right now all four of these show zero because this is a clean demo environment. In a live account with active clients, you'd see real numbers here. The workflow is simple: if High Risk is nonzero, address those clients first. If Recent Intakes is nonzero, new clients need initial contact."

**Pause:** 3 seconds.

---

### Step 4: FMLA Tracker Panel

**Narration:**
"Scrolling down, the next major section is the FMLA Tracker panel. FMLA — Family and Medical Leave Act — casework is deadline-driven. Missing an FMLA deadline can create real problems for your clients, so this panel is built into the Dashboard so you never have to go looking for it."

**Action:**
Scroll down slowly until the FMLA Tracker section is centered on screen. Pause.

**Narration (continuing):**
"The description explains what the full FMLA workspace covers: employer packets, provider certifications, document status, communication history, and linked reminders. The 'Open FMLA Tracker' button takes you directly into that workspace. We're not going to click into it right now — we'll stay on the Dashboard."

**Action:**
Point at the "Open FMLA Tracker" button. Do not click it.

**Narration (continuing):**
"Below the button, you'll see six status counts. These are live numbers — they reflect the current state of your FMLA cases. Let me go through them."

**Action:**
Move cursor slowly across the six status labels: Active → Due In 7 Days → Missing Paperwork → Needs Follow-Up → Approved → Denied.

**Narration (continuing):**
"'Active' shows how many FMLA cases are currently open. Right now that shows 1, which means there's one active FMLA case in this demo environment. 'Due In 7 Days' — this is the one to watch every morning. Any nonzero number here means you have a deadline coming up this week. 'Missing Paperwork' flags cases where required documents haven't been received yet. 'Needs Follow-Up' catches cases waiting on a confirmation. 'Approved' and 'Denied' are completed statuses."

**Action:**
Point briefly at each label as you name it. Pause on "Due In 7 Days" and "Missing Paperwork" for a beat longer to reinforce their importance.

**Narration (continuing):**
"Here's the important part: every one of these status labels is a clickable link. If you click 'Due In 7 Days,' it doesn't just open the FMLA tracker — it opens it pre-filtered to show only cases with deadlines in the next seven days. Same for 'Needs Follow-Up' — it opens filtered to cases in a Confirmation pending status. This turns the Dashboard into a launchpad, not just a display."

**Action:**
Hover cursor over "Due In 7 Days" label — show it is interactive. Do not click.

**Pause:** 3 seconds.

---

### Step 5: Notes, Docs, Bookmarks, and Resources

**Narration:**
"Continuing down the page, you'll see four compact sections side by side: Notes, Docs, Bookmarks, and Resources. Each one has a count badge and a button to add new items. Right now they all show zero because the demo account doesn't have anything pinned yet."

**Action:**
Pan cursor slowly across the four section headings: Notes → Docs → Bookmarks → Resources.

**Narration (continuing):**
"These sections are your personal quick-reference area pinned directly to the Dashboard. Think of them as a stickable workspace. Notes is for short text reminders or information you want visible. Docs is for documents you want quick access to. Bookmarks lets you save important links or case references. Resources is for files you want to keep handy."

**Action:**
Move cursor to each section heading as you describe it.

**Narration (continuing):**
"Important distinction: these are not the same as case-specific documents that live inside individual client records in Case Management. These are Dashboard-level items — things you want visible regardless of which client you're working on. A referral phone number, a copy of the intake form template, a link to a partner agency — that kind of material lives here."

**Pause:** 2 seconds.

---

### Step 6: Supervisor Mode

**Narration:**
"Below the quick-reference sections, you'll see the Supervisor Mode callout. This section is visible because the logged-in user is an Admin / Supervisor. If you were logged in as a standard case manager, this section would not appear — or it would appear differently."

**Action:**
Scroll to the Supervisor Mode section. Center it on screen. Pause.

**Narration (continuing):**
"The panel explains that team-level oversight lives in a separate supervisor workspace. The standard Dashboard is for your individual caseload. The Supervisor Dashboard — which you access by clicking 'Open Supervisor Dashboard' — is where you review caseload pressure across your team, overdue work, high-risk trends across all case managers, and team follow-through. We're not going to click into it in this walkthrough, but know that it's one click away from here."

**Action:**
Point at the "Open Supervisor Dashboard" button. Do not click.

**Pause:** 2 seconds.

**Action:**

**Narration (continuing):**

**Narration (continuing):**

## Unclear Elements — Confirm Before Finalizing

The following items were visible but could not be fully verified from Dashboard inspection alone. Confirm before training goes live:

1. **"9 Modules" vs. 12 visible cards** — The Available Services heading reads "9 Modules" but 12 module cards are visible. Clarify whether this is a count of primary modules, a filtered set, or a display bug before explaining it in training.

2. **UR navigation link** — The expanded nav includes a `/ur` link with no visible text label. Likely Utilization Review. Confirm what this module is named and what it does so it can be included in training materials.

3. **Notes / Docs / Bookmarks / Resources add buttons** — These appear as unlabeled icon buttons. Confirm whether they are "+" add buttons or open a modal. This affects whether you can demonstrate the feature live.

4. **"Missing Paperwork" filter** — Unlike other FMLA status links that include a URL parameter, Missing Paperwork links to `/fmla` without a filter. Confirm whether the FMLA module applies an internal default filter or whether this is a gap.

5. **Supervisor Mode visibility for non-supervisors** — Confirm whether the Supervisor Mode section is hidden entirely for Case Manager role accounts or only the button is disabled. This affects how you describe it to mixed-role audiences.
