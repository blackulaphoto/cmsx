# SUD and Mental Health Group Topics

This list summarizes common psychoeducation and skills groups used in SUD and mental health treatment programs.[web:2][web:4][web:9][web:13]

---

## 1. Understanding Addiction as a Disease

**Description**  
Introduces addiction as a chronic brain-based disorder rather than a moral failing, and what this means for treatment and recovery.[web:9][web:13]

- Define addiction and the disease model  
- Biology, psychology, and environment interactions  
- What “chronic, relapsing” means for expectations of recovery  

## 2. How Drugs and Alcohol Affect the Brain and Body

**Description**  
Explains the neurobiology of substances (reward pathways, tolerance, withdrawal) and physical health impacts to build insight and motivation.[web:1][web:9]

- Brain reward system and dopamine  
- Tolerance, dependence, and withdrawal  
- Long‑term health consequences and benefits of abstinence  

## 3. The Cycle of Addiction

**Description**  
Covers the progression from experimental use to dependency and the repeating binge–crash–resolve–relapse cycle.[web:9][web:13]

- Stages of use and escalation  
- How the cycle keeps people stuck  
- Identifying where you are in the cycle today  

## 4. Stages of Change and Motivation for Recovery

**Description**  
Uses the stages-of-change framework (precontemplation through maintenance) to normalize ambivalence and support movement toward change.[web:3][web:7]

- Overview of stages of change  
- Normalizing mixed feelings about sobriety  
- Ways to strengthen motivation and “decisional balance”  

## 5. Impact of Addiction on Relationships and Family Systems

**Description**  
Explores how substance use affects trust, communication, roles, and boundaries within families and close relationships.[web:9][web:13]

- Common roles in families affected by SUD  
- Trust, secrecy, and communication breakdowns  
- Healthy steps toward repair and accountability  

## 6. Co‑Occurring Disorders: When Mental Health and SUD Overlap

**Description**  
Explains how depression, anxiety, PTSD, and other conditions interact with substance use and why integrated treatment matters.[web:4][web:8][web:9]

- What “co‑occurring” means  
- How mental health symptoms can trigger use and vice versa  
- Benefits of coordinated care and honest symptom reporting  

## 7. Understanding Cravings and Triggers

**Description**  
Defines internal and external triggers, explains craving as a time‑limited experience, and prepares for later coping skills groups.[web:1][web:3][web:11]

- Types of triggers (people, places, things, feelings, thoughts)  
- How cravings show up in the body and mind  
- Why “white‑knuckling” alone is not enough  

## 8. Shame, Guilt, and Stigma in Addiction

**Description**  
Differentiates shame from guilt, examines internalized stigma, and connects self‑compassion to sustainable recovery.[web:7][web:9]

- Shame vs guilt and how each shows up  
- Impact of stigma from others and self‑stigma  
- Self‑compassion as an alternative to self‑attack  

## 9. Making Sense of Your Use History

**Description**  
Helps participants organize their substance use history into a coherent narrative to increase insight and identify patterns.[web:15]

- First use, progression, “bottoms,” and turning points  
- Patterns in people/places/feelings surrounding use  
- Lessons learned and themes you want to change  

## 10. Addiction, Legal Issues, and Consequences

**Description**  
Reviews legal, occupational, and financial consequences of substance use and connects accountability to recovery planning.[web:11][web:13]

- Common legal and work‑related impacts of SUD  
- Short‑term vs long‑term consequences  
- Steps toward legal, financial, and relational repair  

## 11. Lapse vs Relapse

**Description**  
Teaches the distinction between a slip and a full relapse and focuses on rapid course‑correction instead of all‑or‑nothing thinking.[web:3][web:11]

- Definitions of lapse and relapse  
- “Abstinence violation effect” and black‑and‑white thinking  
- How to respond after a slip without giving up  

## 12. Early Warning Signs of Relapse

**Description**  
Builds awareness of subtle behavioral, emotional, and thinking changes that typically precede relapse.[web:3][web:7][web:11]

- Common warning signs (isolation, secrecy, romanticizing use)  
- Personal early‑warning signatures  
- Acting early instead of waiting for crisis  

## 13. High‑Risk Situations and Refusal Skills

**Description**  
Identifies risky situations and teaches concrete ways to say “no” and exit safely.[web:3][web:11]

- People, places, and events that raise risk  
- Assertive refusal language and body posture  
- Planning exits and backup supports  

## 14. Coping with Cravings: Skills Practice

**Description**  
Provides tools like urge surfing and delay–distract–decide, with in‑group practice.[web:3][web:8][web:11]

- Normalizing and rating cravings  
- Urge surfing and grounding skills  
- Building a personal craving‑coping toolbox  

## 15. Building a Relapse Prevention Plan

**Description**  
Guides members to create individualized, written relapse prevention and crisis plans.[web:3][web:7][web:15]

- Personal triggers and warning signs list  
- People to call and places to go when at risk  
- Steps for getting back on track after a slip  

## 16. Weekends, Holidays, and Special Events

**Description**  
Focuses on times when routines change and exposure to substances or stress is higher.[web:11]

- Identifying upcoming high‑risk dates and events  
- Sober plans, exit strategies, and accountability partners  
- Coping with FOMO, loneliness, and family stress  

## 17. Managing Boredom and Restlessness

**Description**  
Addresses boredom as a major but often overlooked relapse trigger and promotes values‑based, sober activities.[web:10][web:11]

- How boredom shows up for each person  
- Unhelpful vs helpful ways of dealing with boredom  
- Brainstorming low‑cost, sober activities and structure  

## 18. Sleep, Fatigue, and Relapse Risk

**Description**  
Explores sleep problems common in recovery and how sleep deprivation increases relapse vulnerability.[web:1][web:2][web:11]

- How sleep and mood/cravings interact  
- Basic sleep hygiene habits  
- When to seek medical evaluation for sleep issues  

## 19. Substance Use and Suicide/Crisis Safety Planning

**Description**  
Links SUD to suicide risk, normalizes talking about it, and develops safety plans including crisis resources.[web:7][web:11]

- Risk factors: intoxication, withdrawal, impulsivity  
- Warning signs in self and others  
- Creating and sharing a written safety plan  

## 20. Long‑Term Recovery Maintenance

**Description**  
Addresses life‑long recovery tasks: monitoring complacency, ongoing growth, and protecting gains.[web:3][web:7][web:11]

- “Complacency traps” and overconfidence  
- Continuing care: therapy, groups, sponsors, supports  
- Balancing recovery with work, family, and goals  

## 21. CBT Basics: Thoughts, Feelings, Behaviors

**Description**  
Introduces the CBT model and shows how thoughts, emotions, and behaviors interact in addiction and mental health.[web:4][web:8][web:12]

- The CBT triangle and personal examples  
- How automatic thoughts drive urges and mood  
- Using CBT to create alternative responses  

## 22. Challenging Unhelpful Thoughts

**Description**  
Teaches common thinking errors and basic cognitive restructuring steps.[web:4][web:8]

- Common cognitive distortions in SUD and depression/anxiety  
- Evidence‑for/evidence‑against exercise  
- Creating balanced replacement thoughts  

## 23. Emotional Awareness and Naming Feelings

**Description**  
Helps clients increase emotional vocabulary and recognize body cues linked to feelings.[web:1][web:7][web:8]

- Differentiating primary vs secondary emotions  
- Body cues for different feelings  
- Using emotion words instead of numbing or acting out  

## 24. Distress Tolerance Skills

**Description**  
Focuses on surviving emotional crises without making them worse, using DBT‑informed tools.[web:4][web:7]

- When to use distress tolerance vs problem solving  
- Quick “in‑the‑moment” skills (temperature, breathing, movement)  
- Safe distractions and sensory‑based self‑soothing  

## 25. Emotion Regulation Skills for Recovery

**Description**  
Builds skills to reduce vulnerability to intense emotion (sleep, nutrition, exercise, positive activities).[web:1][web:2][web:4]

- Emotion check‑ins and tracking  
- Reducing vulnerability (sleep, food, medications, routine)  
- Planning daily positive experiences  

## 26. Anger Management and Conflict De‑Escalation

**Description**  
Explores anger triggers and skills to respond without aggression or use.[web:1][web:4][web:7]

- Anger warning signs and body cues  
- Anger “iceberg” (what’s underneath)  
- Time‑outs, “I” statements, and problem‑solving steps  

## 27. Managing Anxiety and Panic in Recovery

**Description**  
Provides psychoeducation and basic CBT strategies for anxiety, emphasizing non‑substance coping.[web:4][web:8]

- What anxiety is and isn’t  
- Avoidance vs gradual exposure  
- Breathing, grounding, and realistic thinking  

## 28. Managing Depression and Low Motivation

**Description**  
Covers behavioral activation and CBT tools to address depression that often co‑occurs with SUD.[web:4][web:8][web:9]

- Depression symptoms vs normal sadness  
- Activity scheduling and “do first, feel later”  
- How isolation and rumination increase use risk  

## 29. Mindfulness for Cravings and Emotions

**Description**  
Introduces mindfulness as present‑moment, non‑judgmental awareness and practices brief exercises.[web:1][web:2][web:11]

- What mindfulness is (and is not)  
- Short practices: mindful breathing, body scan, five senses  
- Using mindfulness with cravings and strong emotions  

## 30. Problem‑Solving and Decision‑Making Skills

**Description**  
Teaches a structured approach to solving practical and interpersonal problems without substances.[web:4][web:8]

- Defining the problem clearly  
- Brainstorming options and weighing pros/cons  
- Choosing and testing a plan, then reviewing outcomes  

## 31. Healthy Communication and Assertiveness

**Description**  
Focuses on clear, respectful communication and saying what you need without aggression or passivity.[web:4][web:7]

- Passive, aggressive, and assertive styles  
- Using “I” statements and active listening  
- Practicing assertive requests and boundaries  

## 32. Boundaries and Codependency in Recovery

**Description**  
Explains healthy vs unhealthy boundaries and how codependent patterns can fuel relapse.[web:7][web:9]

- Types of boundaries (physical, emotional, time, money)  
- Signs of codependency and enabling  
- Setting and maintaining limits with others  

## 33. Loneliness, Isolation, and Building Connection

**Description**  
Addresses loneliness as a risk factor and explores ways to build safe, supportive connections.[web:10][web:11]

- How isolation shows up in your life  
- Balancing alone time vs connection  
- Practical steps to meet supportive people  

## 34. Family Roles, Communication, and Recovery

**Description**  
Looks at family interaction patterns and ways to shift conversations toward support instead of conflict.[web:7][web:9][web:13]

- Common family roles in addiction  
- Unhelpful communication cycles (blame, shutdown, escalation)  
- Scripts for difficult conversations about recovery  

## 35. Peer Support, 12‑Step, and Alternatives

**Description**  
Introduces different recovery fellowships and how to get the most out of meetings or groups.[web:11][web:13]

- Types of peer‑support programs (12‑step, SMART, etc.)  
- What to expect at a meeting  
- Choosing a group, sponsor/mentor, or peer support  

## 36. Romantic Relationships, Intimacy, and Recovery

**Description**  
Explores how substance use and mental health affect intimacy and how to approach dating/relationships in early recovery.[web:7][web:9]

- Common relationship pitfalls early in recovery  
- Guidelines and boundaries around dating  
- Building emotional intimacy without substances  

## 37. Communicating About Triggers with Loved Ones

**Description**  
Teaches how to explain personal triggers and safety needs to supportive people in a concrete way.[web:3][web:7]

- Identifying what others need to know  
- Scripts for asking for support  
- Responding when others do not understand  

## 38. Rebuilding Trust and Making Amends

**Description**  
Connects relapse prevention with honesty, consistency, and thoughtful amends where appropriate.[web:7][web:11]

- What trust is and how it’s rebuilt  
- Difference between apologies and amends  
- When making contact may not be safe or appropriate  

## 39. Work/School Issues and Recovery

**Description**  
Addresses disclosure, accommodations, stress, and boundaries related to work or school.[web:6][web:11]

- Pros/cons of telling employers/teachers about recovery  
- Handling triggers at work/school  
- Time management and self‑care around responsibilities  

## 40. Social Media, Technology, and Triggers

**Description**  
Explores how online content can trigger cravings, comparison, or mood swings and how to use tech more intentionally.[web:1][web:11]

- Identifying triggering or draining online spaces  
- Boundaries around time, content, and people online  
- Curating feeds to support recovery and mental health  

## 41. Trauma and PTSD in the Context of Addiction

**Description**  
Provides high‑level psychoeducation on trauma, PTSD, and why many people use substances to cope, with emphasis on safety and stabilization.[web:4][web:7][web:9]

- What trauma and PTSD are (and are not)  
- How trauma symptoms can drive substance use  
- Grounding skills and when to seek trauma‑focused therapy  

## 42. Grief, Loss, and Recovery

**Description**  
Addresses grief related to death, relationships, health, and “lost years” from addiction.[web:7][web:9]

- Types of loss (people, opportunities, self‑image)  
- Myths about “getting over” grief  
- Healthy ways to express and process grief  

## 43. Identity, Self‑Esteem, and Rebuilding Self‑Worth

**Description**  
Helps clients separate their identity from their illness and build a more compassionate self‑view.[web:7][web:9]

- Messages you absorbed about yourself from addiction and others  
- Challenging shame‑based self‑statements  
- Building a strengths‑based recovery identity  

## 44. Values Clarification and Life Direction

**Description**  
Uses values work to help clients define what truly matters and align daily actions with those values.[web:2][web:8]

- Distinguishing values from goals and feelings  
- Identifying top personal values (family, honesty, health, etc.)  
- Small daily actions in line with chosen values  

## 45. Spirituality, Meaning, and Purpose in Recovery

**Description**  
Explores spirituality broadly (not tied to any specific religion) as a source of meaning and resilience.[web:11][web:13]

- Definitions of spirituality and meaning  
- Ways people experience connection (nature, service, community)  
- Personal practices that support a sense of purpose  

## 46. Telling Your Recovery Story Safely

**Description**  
Supports participants in sharing parts of their story in a way that is empowering and boundaried.[web:1][web:7]

- What you want to share vs keep private  
- Focusing on strengths and growth, not just damage  
- Practicing short “recovery story” introductions  

## 47. Physical Health, Nutrition, and Exercise in Recovery

**Description**  
Explains how movement and nutrition support mood stability, energy, and relapse prevention.[web:1][web:2][web:11]

- Effects of substances and withdrawal on the body  
- Simple, realistic steps toward better nutrition  
- Low‑barrier ways to move your body safely  

## 48. Daily Structure, Time Management, and Routines

**Description**  
Shows how predictable routines reduce risk and support mental health and recovery progress.[web:2][web:3][web:11]

- Benefits of a daily schedule in early recovery  
- Building anchors: wake time, meals, meetings, movement  
- Planning for unstructured time (evenings, weekends)  

## 49. Self‑Care and Self‑Compassion Practices

**Description**  
Normalizes self‑care as essential, not selfish, and introduces practical, low‑cost options.[web:1][web:11]

- Myths about self‑care and “deserving” rest  
- Quick daily practices (hygiene, rest, hobbies, connection)  
- Using self‑talk that is realistic and kind  

## 50. Aftercare Planning and Community Resources

**Description**  
Helps participants build a concrete plan for supports, services, and routines after discharge.[web:6][web:11][web:13]

- Mapping out appointments, groups, and supports post‑program  
- Housing, transportation, employment/education resources  
- What to do in the first 24–72 hours if cravings spike or stress hits
